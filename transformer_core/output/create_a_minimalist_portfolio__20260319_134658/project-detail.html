Nexus Design System

Building a scalable foundation for consistent, accessible digital products across an organization.

Project Overview

The Nexus Design System was created to solve a critical challenge faced by a growing tech company: inconsistent user experiences across multiple products and development teams. Without a unified design language, each team was building components from scratch, leading to accessibility issues, brand inconsistency, and development inefficiency.

My role involved leading the design system initiative from research and planning through to implementation and developer adoption. The system needed to serve both designers using Figma and developers working with React, while maintaining strict accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1 AA).

The result was a comprehensive design system that reduced development time by 40%, improved accessibility compliance across all products, and created a consistent brand experience for millions of users.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Adoption Resistance

Development teams were accustomed to building custom components and hesitant to adopt a centralized system that might limit their flexibility.

Solution: Created comprehensive documentation with migration guides, offered workshops and office hours, and demonstrated clear time-saving benefits through pilot projects with early-adopter teams.

Challenge: Maintaining Consistency

Ensuring all teams used the design system correctly and consistently across different products and codebases.

Solution: Implemented automated linting rules, created a contribution model for adding new components, and established regular design system office hours for team support.

Challenge: Performance Optimization

The design system needed to be lightweight and performant while providing comprehensive component coverage.

Solution: Implemented code splitting and tree shaking, created a modular architecture allowing teams to import only needed components, and optimized bundle size through careful dependency management.
