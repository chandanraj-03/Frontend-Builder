Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 26, 2024

At Digital Horizon, we are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains our practices in detail.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information to provide better services to our readers and improve our content. The types of information we collect include:

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, and password.
  • Newsletter Subscriptions: When you subscribe to our newsletters, we collect your email address and subscription preferences.
  • Comments and Contributions: When you comment on articles or submit content, we collect the content you provide and associated metadata.
  • Contact Information: When you contact our editorial team or customer support, we collect your name, email address, and the content of your message.
  • Survey Responses: When you participate in reader surveys, we collect your responses and demographic information you choose to provide.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage Data: We collect information about your interactions with our website, including pages visited, time spent, articles read, and navigation patterns.
  • Device Information: We collect information about the device you use to access our services, including device type, operating system, browser type, IP address, and unique device identifiers.
  • Location Information: We may collect approximate location information based on your IP address to provide localized content and analyze geographic readership patterns.
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We use cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies to collect information about your browsing activities.

1.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Social Media Platforms: If you connect your social media accounts, we may receive information from those platforms according to their privacy policies.
  • Analytics Providers: We receive aggregated analytics data from services like Google Analytics and Parse.ly.
  • Advertising Partners: We may receive information from advertising partners about the effectiveness of campaigns.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To provide, maintain, and improve our content and services, including personalized recommendations and saved reading lists.
  • Communication: To send you newsletters, updates about new articles, responses to your inquiries, and important service announcements.
  • Personalization: To customize your experience by showing you content and advertising that may be of interest to you based on your reading history and preferences.
  • Analytics: To understand how our content is used, analyze reader engagement, and improve our editorial strategy and website functionality.
  • Advertising: To display relevant advertisements and measure their effectiveness. We use contextual advertising based on article content rather than personal profiling.
  • Security: To detect, prevent, and address technical issues, fraud, or other malicious activity.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes.

3. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use several types of cookies and tracking technologies:

3.1 Essential Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Necessary for website functionality, such as maintaining your login state and shopping cart.
  • Security Cookies: Used to detect malicious activity and protect user accounts.

3.2 Analytics Cookies

  • Google Analytics: Helps us understand how visitors interact with our website. We use anonymized IP addresses and do not share individual user data with Google.
  • Parse.ly: Provides insights into content performance and reader engagement patterns.

3.3 Advertising Cookies

  • Contextual Advertising: We work with advertising partners who use cookies to serve ads based on the content you're viewing rather than personal behavior tracking.
  • Frequency Capping: Cookies help ensure you don't see the same ad too many times.

3.4 Your Cookie Choices

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. However, if you disable essential cookies, some features of our website may not function properly.

We provide a cookie consent banner that allows you to customize your cookie preferences when you first visit our website.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We share information with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our website, conducting analytics, processing payments, or sending communications.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or other legal process, or if necessary to protect our rights, property, or safety, or the rights, property, or safety of others.
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, user information may be transferred as part of that transaction.
  • Aggregated Data: We may share aggregated, anonymized data that does not identify individual users for research, analytics, or marketing purposes.

All third parties with whom we share personal information are contractually obligated to protect that information and use it only for the purposes for which it was disclosed.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Employee training on data protection best practices
  • Incident response procedures for potential data breaches

While we strive to protect your information, no security system is impenetrable, and we cannot guarantee the absolute security of our systems.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You can request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: You can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: You can request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Opt-Out: You can opt out of marketing communications by clicking the "unsubscribe" link in any email or adjusting your account settings.
  • Data Portability: You can request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Restriction: You can request restriction of processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: You can object to processing based on legitimate interests.

To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@digitalhorizon.com. We will respond to your request within 30 days, subject to verification of your identity.

7. International Data Transfers

Digital Horizon is based in the United States, but we have readers and contributors worldwide. When you use our services, your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States or other countries where our service providers operate.

We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for international data transfers, including standard contractual clauses approved by the European Commission and other mechanisms as required by applicable law.

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under 16, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

If you believe we might have any information from or about a child under 16, please contact us at privacy@digitalhorizon.com.

9. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

Our retention periods vary depending on the type of data and its purpose:

  • Account information: Retained while your account is active and for 3 years after deactivation
  • Newsletter subscriptions: Retained while you are subscribed and for 1 year after unsubscribing
  • Comment data: Retained indefinitely as part of article discussions
  • Analytics data: Aggregated and anonymized after 26 months
  • Server logs: Retained for 90 days for security purposes

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy.

If we make material changes, we will provide notice through our website, email, or other appropriate means before the changes take effect. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about our privacy practices.
